Environmental Monitoring and Water Management System
Software Requirements Specification (SRS)
1. System Overview

The Environmental Monitoring and Water Management System is designed to monitor environmental activities, manage water quality, and control a filtration and distribution process.

The system will use a webcam positioned above the monitored area to observe environmental conditions such as tree cover and excavation activities. It will also integrate water quality sensors and filtration hardware to detect contaminants, filter water, generate energy through turbines, and distribute clean water to nearby homes.

The software will process sensor data and video feeds in real time, generate alerts for suspicious activities, and provide operational insights through a statistics dashboard.

2. System Modules
2.1 Environmental Monitoring Module

This module monitors environmental activities within the observed area.

Features

Tree Cover Monitoring

Detect reduction in tree density using image analysis.

Estimate percentage loss of tree cover.

Generate alerts when deforestation exceeds a predefined threshold.

Excavation Detection

Detect formation of deep holes or excavation activity.

Use visual comparison from the webcam feed to identify terrain changes.

Activity Alert System

Identify suspicious human or machine activities.

Send notifications to responsible authorities or community members.

2.2 Water Quality Monitoring Module

This module monitors chemical composition and quality of water passing through the system.

Features

Chemical Detection

Collect water quality data from sensors.

Monitor parameters such as:

pH level

Turbidity

Chemical contamination levels.

Treatment Recommendations

Analyze chemical readings.

Recommend appropriate treatment processes (e.g., filtration, neutralization, purification).

2.3 Filtration System Management Module

This module controls the water filtration and debris removal processes.

Features

Debris Removal System

Detect accumulation of debris in water channels.

Automatically activate a net mechanism to collect large debris.

Trigger automatic dumping when the net reaches capacity.

Filtration Progress Monitoring

Track water flow through filters.

Monitor filter efficiency and clogging status.

Notify operators when filters require cleaning or replacement.

Water Distribution Management

Manage delivery of filtered water through pipelines to residential areas.

Monitor flow rates and supply levels.

Route Optimization

Determine efficient distribution paths to reduce water loss and energy usage.

2.4 Energy Generation and Statistics Module

This module monitors energy generated by turbines and system performance.

Features

Turbine Monitoring

Measure electricity produced by turbines installed in the water flow system.

Track energy production over time.

Daily Statistics Dashboard

Display:

Total water filtered per day

Energy generated

Water distributed to households

System operational status

3. User Interface Requirements

The system interface should provide an intuitive dashboard for monitoring and control.

Interface Components

Real-Time Video Feed

Display live webcam feed of the monitored environment.

Alert Notification System

Show alerts for:

Tree cutting

Excavation

Suspicious activity

Water contamination

Water Quality Panel

Display chemical readings and sensor data.

Provide treatment recommendations.

Filtration System Status Panel

Show filter condition.

Display water flow rate and debris removal status.

Statistics Dashboard

Present daily and historical statistics including:

Water filtered

Electricity generated

Water distribution levels.

4. Hardware Requirements

The system requires the following hardware components:

Webcam

Mounted above the monitored field to capture environmental activity.

Water Quality Sensors

Sensors capable of measuring chemical concentrations and water quality parameters.

Automated Debris Removal System

Motorized net mechanism for collecting and dumping large debris.

Water Turbines

Installed in water flow channels to generate electricity.

Water Distribution Infrastructure

Pipes and electronically controlled valves for delivering water to households.

5. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance

System should process video and sensor data in near real-time.

Reliability

System should operate continuously with minimal downtime.

Scalability

System should support monitoring of multiple environments in the future.

Security

Access to system controls should require authentication.

Usability

Dashboard should be simple and easy to interpret for operators and authorities.
